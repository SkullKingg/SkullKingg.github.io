What I Found Important(extra section I thought was significant)

The Stone Soup analogy is very important even though the general idea is often overlooked. Many programmers would understand that starting your code is one of the most difficult things to do. Even with a full game-plan, once you begin coding, its as if the entire plan was ripped to shreds and set on fire, leaving you clueless once again. This mentality can help programmers, even if they aren't trying to persuade important figures for resources or time. Starting off with the bare minimum, and adding the other processes as "features" in order view the project as a process over time, compared to a stressful assignment that may feel rushed at times.
